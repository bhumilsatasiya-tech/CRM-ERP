1. Getting started

1.1 Logging in

  1. Open your browser at http://localhost:5173.
  2. You'll see a Sign-in card.
  3. Enter your email and password. The first administrator account is:
    Emailadmin@crm-erp.local
    PasswordChangeMe@123
  4. Click Sign in. The system takes you to the Dashboard.
Change the default password. The first admin is flagged must change password. Set a real one before adding other users.

1.2 First-time setup (recommended order)

  1. Verify the two seeded companies exist (Company Management → Companies). You should see COA — Company A and COB — Company B.
  2. Set company-level defaults (System → Settings) — currency, payment terms, document footer text.
  3. Create user accounts for your team (Users & Roles → Users) and assign appropriate roles.
  4. Add partners (CRM → Partners) — your real clients, suppliers, manufacturers.
  5. Add products (Product Management → Products) — your real SKUs.
  6. Enter opening stock (Inventory & Stock → Adjustments) — set today's quantities.
  7. You're now ready for daily transactions: purchase, sales, transfers.

1.3 The screen, top to bottom

Top bar
Always visible at the top. Holds the active company switcher (left) and your name + Sign out (right). The company you choose here filters every list and form on the rest of the screen.
Left sidebar
Grouped menu items. Click a group to expand its sub-menus. The sidebar can be collapsed for more workspace using the arrow at the bottom of the panel.
Main area
Whatever you click in the sidebar opens here — a list, a form, or a report. All edit screens have Back, Save, and (where applicable) workflow buttons like Approve, Post, Cancel at the top right.

2. The top bar

2.1 Active company switcher

The system runs two companies side-by-side: Company A (export) and Company B (supplying). Everything you see — partners, products, orders, invoices, stock — belongs to one specific company.

Use the dropdown to switch:

  • Pick a company from the list (the active one is shown with its code on the right).
  • The page reloads automatically; lists and forms now show data for the new company.
  • Each company tracks its own document numbers (PO/2026/00001 in Company A is independent of PO/2026/00001 in Company B).
Tip: if you don't see a record you expected, check the top bar — you may be in the wrong company.

2.2 Your name and Sign out

  • Your full name is displayed.
  • Click Sign out to end the session.

3. Dashboard

The Dashboard is your landing page after signing in. It shows:

  • Your name and the active company.
  • Your roles (super-admin, admin, manager, etc.).
  • Quick links to Manage Companies, Manage Users, and Manage Roles.

As more modules are activated, the Dashboard will grow into a KPI panel (open invoices, pending orders, low stock alerts, etc.).

4. Company Management

This is where you set up the legal entities, branches, and warehouses that hold your operations.

4.1 Companies (list)

Sidebar: Company Management → Companies

What you see: a table of all companies you have access to, with code, name, type, GST, branch count, warehouse count, and active status.

Filters at the top:

  • Search — searches code, name, legal name, GST.
  • Type — Export / Supplying / Trading / Other.
  • Status — Active or Inactive only.
  • Refresh — re-pull from the server.
  • + New company (top right) — start a new one.

On each row you can:

  • Click Branches to manage that company's branches.
  • Click Warehouses to manage that company's storage locations.
  • Click the pencil icon to edit, the trash icon to delete (only if no branches / warehouses exist).

4.2 Add / edit a company

The form is grouped into clear sections:

IdentityCode (short alphanumeric, e.g. COA), Name, Legal name, Type
Currency & FYCurrency (defaults to INR), Fiscal year start date
Tax / regulatoryGST, PAN, CIN, IEC, Registration number
ContactEmail, phone, website
AddressLine 1, Line 2, City, State, Country, Postal code
ActiveToggle to enable or disable this company

Click Create company (or Save changes) to commit. Cancel returns to the list.

4.3 Branches

Sidebar → Companies → click Branches on a company row.

Each company can have multiple branches (offices, regional centers). One can be flagged as the Head Office.

Fields: Code, Name, Head office (toggle), Email, Phone, GST at this branch, full Address, Active toggle.

Use branches to track addresses for invoices that ship from a different location than the registered head office.

4.4 Warehouses

Sidebar → Companies → click Warehouses on a company row.

Warehouses are physical (or logical) locations where stock is held. Pre-seeded warehouses for each company:

  • WH-FG — Finished Goods
  • WH-RM — Raw Material

When creating a warehouse, choose its type:

TypeUsed for
FinishedFinished goods ready to sell
RawRaw materials waiting for production
PackagingPackaging supplies (boxes, labels, films)
QuarantineStock pending QC release
TransitStock currently moving between locations
RejectRejected / damaged stock to be written off

One warehouse per company can be marked Default — used as the auto-suggested location on new documents.

5. CRM — Partners

Partners are everyone you transact with: clients, suppliers, manufacturers, and employee-vendors. They're stored in one master table with a Type field.

5.1 Partners (list)

Sidebar: CRM → Partners

What you see: all partners for the active company, with code, name, type, segment, GST, currency, credit limit, contacts count, and active flag.

Filters at the top:

  • Search (code / name / GST / phone / email).
  • Type — Client / Supplier / Manufacturer / Employee / Other.
  • Segment — B2B / B2C / Distributor / OEM / Other.
  • Status — Active / Inactive.

Click the pencil icon to edit, trash to delete. Click + New partner to add one.

5.2 New / edit partner — General tab

Sections of the General tab:

IdentityCode, Name, Legal name, Type, Segment, Industry, "Is a company" toggle
ContactEmail, Phone, Mobile, Website
TaxGST, PAN, Tax treatment (Registered / Unregistered / Composition / SEZ / Overseas)
FinanceCurrency, Credit limit, Credit days, Payment terms (days), Opening balance + type (Debit/Credit)
FlagsActive, Blacklisted (with reason)
NotesFree-text notes
You must save the partner first before adding contacts, addresses, or bank accounts. The other tabs unlock once the General tab is saved.

5.3 Contacts tab

Multiple human contacts per partner — sales contact, accounts contact, owner, etc.

Click + New contact to open the modal:

  • Name (required), Designation, Department
  • Email, Phone, Mobile
  • Primary toggle — only one contact can be primary; setting another removes the flag from the previous one
  • Active toggle, Notes

5.4 Addresses tab

Multiple addresses per partner, classified by type:

  • billing — used on invoices (To address)
  • shipping — used on dispatch documents
  • registered — registered office for legal correspondence
  • branch — additional locations

For each address: contact name, phone, email, full street address, optional GST at this address (useful when the partner has different GST numbers for different states), Primary toggle (one primary per type per partner).

5.5 Bank accounts tab

Per-partner bank accounts — used for supplier payments and customer remittance tracking.

Fields: Bank name, Branch, Account holder, Account number, Account type (savings / current / corporate), IFSC (India), SWIFT and IBAN (international), Currency, Bank country. Mark one Primary; that becomes the default for payments to this partner.

Account numbers are partially masked in lists for security (e.g. •••••••1234); the full number is shown only on the edit form.

6.1 Products (list)

Sidebar: Product Management → Products

Each row shows: code (SKU), name, type, category, default unit, HSN, tax %, cost, price, active flag.

Filters:

  • Search (code / name / barcode / HSN)
  • Type (Raw / Finished / Packaging / Consumable / Service / Other)
  • Category
  • Status (Active / Inactive)

6.2 New / edit product — 4 tabs

6.3 Categories

Sidebar: Product Management → Categories

Hierarchical (tree). One company has its own category tree.

To create a child category: open the modal, pick a Parent from the dropdown, set Code + Name. Children indent under their parent in the list view.

You can't delete a category that still has children or products — reassign or delete those first.

7. Inventory & Stock

This is where you see and manage physical stock. Every stock movement (purchase receipts, sales dispatches, adjustments, transfers) creates a row in the Stock Ledger automatically.

7.1 Current stock

Sidebar: Inventory & Stock → Current stock

A pivot view showing one row per (product × warehouse) combination with the current balance and value.

The header shows Total stock value across the active company.

Filter by warehouse to focus on one location. Click a row's expand arrow to see batches that make up the total (with batch numbers and expiry dates).

Tip: If a row's balance is unexpectedly negative, look at the Stock Ledger filtered by that product to see which document drove it negative.

7.2 Stock ledger

Sidebar: Inventory & Stock → Stock ledger

The complete history of every stock movement, newest first.

Columns: Posted at, Type, Reference, Product, Warehouse, Batch, Qty (signed), Balance after, Rate, Value.

Movement types:

TypeSignWhere it comes from
opening+Initial opening balance entries
in+Purchase GRN, manufacturing output (future)
out−Sales invoice posting, manufacturing consumption
transfer_in+Receiving warehouse on a transfer
transfer_out−Sending warehouse on a transfer
adjustment±Manual stock adjustment after physical count, damage, etc.

The ledger is append-only — rows are never edited or deleted. Cancellations create a reversal row marked with a red reversal tag (and the original row gets a reversed tag).

Filters: reference search, product autocomplete, movement type, date range.

7.3 Adjustments

Sidebar: Inventory & Stock → Adjustments

Use adjustments to correct stock after a physical count, write off damage, or record opening balances.

Workflow:

1
Draft — fill the form and save. Nothing posted yet.
2
Submit — flag as ready for approval (cannot be edited after).
3
Approve — system writes the ledger rows. The stock balance changes immediately.
4
Cancel — at any step. If already approved, every ledger row gets a reversal.

The form: Warehouse, Adjustment date, Reason (Physical count, Damage, Expired, Theft, Production loss, Opening correction, Other), Notes.

Lines: for each product, enter the current qty (or leave 0) and the counted qty. The system computes the difference and applies it. Optional batch / expiry per line.

7.4 Transfers

Sidebar: Inventory & Stock → Transfers

Move stock between warehouses (within the same company).

Workflow:

1
Draft — fill source warehouse, destination warehouse, lines.
2
Send — system writes transfer_out at source. Goods now in transit.
3
Receive — when goods arrive at destination, click Receive. System writes transfer_in. Done.
4
Cancel — at any step. If already sent or received, all ledger rows get reversals.

Source and destination must be different warehouses.

8. Purchase

The procurement pipeline. Each step is a separate document that links to the previous one but is independent — you can also create any of them stand-alone.

8.1 Purchase Orders (PO)

Sidebar: Purchase → Purchase orders

A formal order placed with a supplier, before goods arrive.

The form: Supplier (autosuggest filtered to type=Supplier), Default destination warehouse, Order date, Expected date, Supplier reference, Notes.

Lines: product autosuggest (filtered to raw materials by default), Qty, Rate, Tax %. Subtotal / tax / total are auto-calculated.

Workflow: Draft → Approve → Partial / Received (set automatically as GRNs arrive) → Cancel.

The PO does not move stock — it's an intent document. Stock arrives via the GRN.

8.2 GRNs (Goods Receipt Note)

Sidebar: Purchase → GRNs

The document you create when the truck arrives at your warehouse. This is the document that actually adds stock.

The form: Supplier, Warehouse (destination), GRN date, Supplier invoice number, Supplier invoice date, Vehicle number, LR (transporter receipt) number, Notes.

Lines: product, qty received, rate, batch number, expiry date, manufacturing date, serial number (where applicable).

Workflow:

1
Draft — fill from the truck. Doesn't move stock yet.
2
Receive — system writes stock IN for every line. The matched PO line's "received qty" goes up. The PO becomes Partial or Received accordingly.
3
Cancel — reverses every stock IN row.

8.3 Purchase Invoices (PI)

Sidebar: Purchase → Purchase invoices

The supplier's bill — the document that creates an amount payable to that supplier.

The form: Supplier, Supplier invoice number, Invoice date, Due date, Notes.

Lines: product, qty, rate, tax % — totals computed automatically.

Workflow: Draft → Post → Partially paid / Paid (as supplier payments are recorded — Finance module, future).

The PI does not affect stock — that already happened on the GRN.

9. Sales

The selling pipeline: Quotation → Sales Order → Invoice → Payment.

9.1 Quotations

Sidebar: Sales → Quotations

A formal price offer to a prospective customer. No stock or accounting impact.

The form: Client (autosuggest), Quotation date, Valid until, Reference, Terms & conditions, Notes.

Lines: product, qty, rate, tax %, line discount %.

Workflow:

  • Draft → Approve — when finalized.
  • Convert to SO — once the customer accepts. Creates a Sales Order with the same lines and links the two documents.
  • Cancel — at any step before conversion.

A converted quotation displays a green "→ SO #X" tag in the list and can no longer be edited.

9.2 Sales Orders (SO)

Sidebar: Sales → Sales orders

A confirmed order from a customer. Drives production and dispatch planning.

The form: Client, Ship-from warehouse, Order date, Expected delivery date, Client PO number, Terms, Notes.

Workflow: Draft → Approved → In production / Partial / Invoiced → Cancelled.

Create invoice from SO: on an approved SO, click Create invoice. The invoice form opens with the SO data prefilled and remaining (un-invoiced) lines pre-populated.

The SO does not affect stock — invoices do.

9.3 Invoices & payments

Sidebar: Sales → Invoices

The customer's bill — the document that removes stock and creates a receivable.

The form: Client, Sales Order (optional link), Ship-from warehouse, Invoice date, Due date, Reference, Terms, Notes.

Lines: product, qty, rate, tax %, batch number, expiry date.

Workflow:

1
Draft — review and edit freely.
2
Post — system writes stock OUT for every line. Invoice now has a balance due.
3
Record payment — opens a modal: amount, date, mode (Bank / Cash / Cheque / UPI / Card), reference (cheque #, UTR). Status auto-updates to Partially paid or Paid.
4
Cancel — reverses the stock OUT and marks the invoice cancelled.

Multiple payments can be recorded against one invoice. Deleting a payment subtracts from paid amount and re-evaluates status.

Manage who has access to the system and what they can do.

10.1 Users

Sidebar: Users & Roles → Users

List columns: Name, Email, Phone, Roles (badges), Active toggle, Last login.

Filters: Search, Role, Status.

New user form:

10.2 Roles

Sidebar: Users & Roles → Roles

Pre-seeded roles:

RoleTypical use
super-adminSystem owner. Bypasses all permission checks.
adminFull operational access — can create / approve in every module.
managerRead everywhere; create & update most documents but not delete.
viewerRead-only across the entire app.

Creating a custom role: click + New role, give it a lowercase name (letters / numbers / dashes only — e.g. sales-manager), optional description, and tick the permissions to grant. Permissions are grouped by module; you can tick a whole group at once.

System roles (super-admin, admin, manager, viewer) cannot be renamed or deleted, but you can adjust their permissions.

11. System

11.1 Settings

Sidebar: System → Settings

Key/value configuration values that drive defaults across the app.

Settings are grouped (General, Finance, Documents, Mail, etc.). Each row shows a label, the key, and an inline editor sized to the value type (text, number, toggle, dropdown).

Examples seeded out of the box:

  • Default currency (INR)
  • Date format, Time format, Locale
  • Default payment terms (days)
  • "Prices include tax by default" toggle
  • Invoice footer text
  • "Send quotation by email by default" toggle

Changes save individually — each row has its own Save button.

11.2 Sequences

Sidebar: System → Sequences

Document numbering rules. Each row is one document type per company.

Pre-seeded sequences (per company):

QT — Quotation SO — Sales Order INV — Invoice CN — Credit Note PO — Purchase Order GRN — GRN PI — Purchase Invoice BT — Production Batch SB — Shipping Bill EI — Export Invoice ICI — Inter-Company Invoice ADJ — Stock Adjustment TRF — Stock Transfer

Each sequence has:

PrefixLetters at the start (e.g. SO)
FormatPattern using tokens like {prefix}, {year}, {month}, {number}. Default {prefix}/{year}/{number} → SO/2026/00001
PaddingNumber of digits in the counter (5 = 00001)
Reset periodNever / Yearly (resets to 1 each Jan 1) / Monthly (resets each 1st)
Current numberLast issued number; the next document gets this + 1

The Next preview column shows what the next document number will look like — handy when changing format.

11.3 Audit log

Sidebar: System → Audit log

Every create / update / delete on every important record is logged here.

Each row shows: when, what event (created / updated / deleted), what type of record, what description, and which user did it.

Filters: search description, event type, date range.

Click View on any row to open a side drawer showing the full properties JSON — useful to see exactly which fields changed.

12. End-to-end workflows

12.1 Buying raw material from a supplier

1
Confirm the supplier exists. CRM → Partners. If not, create one with type = Supplier.
2
Create the Purchase Order. Purchase → Purchase orders → + New PO. Pick the supplier, target warehouse, add lines (product / qty / rate / tax). Save → Approve.
3
Truck arrives. Purchase → GRNs → + New GRN. Pick the same supplier and warehouse, fill supplier invoice number, vehicle, LR. Add lines with batch numbers and expiry dates. Save.
4
Click Receive. System writes stock IN to the ledger. The PO updates from Approved → Partial or Received.
5
Verify stock. Inventory & Stock → Current stock — your raw material should be there.
6
(Optional) Record the supplier bill. Purchase → Purchase invoices → + New PI. Pick supplier, fill the supplier invoice number, save → Post. Now you have a payable on file.

12.2 Quoting and selling to a customer

1
Send a quote. Sales → Quotations → + New quotation. Pick the client, add lines (finished products), save → Approve.
2
Customer accepts. Open the approved quotation → click Convert to SO. The Sales Order opens prefilled.
3
Approve the SO. The order is now firm and ready for dispatch / production.
4
Bill the customer. On the SO, click Create invoice. The invoice opens with lines prefilled. Add batch numbers and expiry on each line. Save → Post — this writes stock OUT.
5
Customer pays. Open the invoice → Record payment → enter amount, date, mode (e.g. Bank), reference (UTR). Status flips to Partially paid or Paid.
6
Verify. Inventory & Stock → Current stock — the finished goods balance has dropped. The Sales Order is now Invoiced. The audit log has every step on record.

12.3 Adjusting stock after a count

1
Inventory & Stock → Adjustments → + New adjustment.
2
Pick the warehouse, set Reason = "Physical count", date = today.
3
Add lines: for each product whose count differs, enter the current qty (what the system shows now) and the counted qty (what you actually counted). Difference auto-calculates.
4
Save → Submit → Approve (a separate person should approve, ideally). Stock is now corrected.

12.4 Transferring stock between warehouses

1
Inventory & Stock → Transfers → + New transfer. Pick source and destination warehouses.
2
Add the lines with qty and (if relevant) batch numbers. Save.
3
When the goods leave the source: click Send. Stock goes out of source.
4
When goods arrive at destination: click Receive. Stock comes in at destination. Done.

12.5 Working across both companies

Each company is fully isolated by default — partners, products, stock, and documents in Company A do not affect Company B. To work in the other company:

  1. Click the company switcher in the top bar.
  2. Select the other company.
  3. The page reloads. You're now in the other company's data.

If a partner or product needs to exist in both companies, create it twice (once per company). A future Inter-Company Billing module will let Company B invoice Company A automatically.

14. Frequently asked questions & tips

I can't find a record I just created — where is it?

Check the company switcher in the top bar. Each company has its own separate data. Records you create in Company A won't appear when you're viewing Company B (and vice versa).

Why can't I edit this document?

Most documents become read-only once they leave the draft status. Look at the colored status tag at the top of the form. If it's anything other than draft (or sometimes submitted), the document is locked. Cancel and create a new one if you need to change something.

How do I undo a posted invoice / received GRN / approved adjustment?

Click the Cancel button. The system creates a reversal in the stock ledger so the books balance — both rows remain in history, marked. This keeps the audit trail honest.

Can I delete a partner / product?

Yes (with the trash icon), provided it isn't referenced by any documents. If it is, deactivate it instead — that prevents new use without breaking historical data.

How do I see who changed what and when?

System → Audit log. Every create, update, and delete on every important record is logged with the user, timestamp, and the fields that changed.

The list is empty but I'm sure data exists. What now?

Check, in this order:

  1. The active company in the top bar.
  2. Any active filters at the top of the list (Type, Status, Search).
  3. Click Refresh in case the data was just added.

How do I assign a user to a specific company only?

Currently the seeded admin is attached to both companies. To restrict a user, edit their profile and set their default company; their access scope follows their assigned companies. (More fine-grained per-company permissions arrive in a later release.)

Tips that save time

  • Auto-suggest: on partner / product fields, just start typing — the dropdown filters live.
  • Approve in two clicks: Save → Approve. Most documents have these buttons next to each other.
  • Convert button: on an approved Quotation, the green "Convert to SO" button is the fastest way to start a sales order.
  • Create invoice from SO: on an approved Sales Order, the "Create invoice" button prefills lines from the SO — far faster than typing them again.
  • Recompute totals: totals on every document refresh as soon as you change a qty or rate. No "Recalculate" button needed.
  • Press Esc in any modal to close it without saving.
